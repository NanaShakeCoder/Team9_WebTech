INTRODUCTION

What are natural resources

Natural resources are essential for human survival and development, supplying air, water, soil, and energy. However, unsustainable consumption is causing rapid depletion, leading to climate change, biodiversity loss, pollution, and ecological collapseâ€”threatening both humanity and nature. There are renewable resources which replenish naturally and non-renewable resources which are limited.

THE CAUSES

OVERPOPULATION

As the population grows yearly so does the demand. This requires a huge amount of resources which puts stress on the environments just to meet the demand.

INDUSTRIALIZATION

This process requires land, energy, and raw materials which are in large amounts. This leads to pollution from factories, resource depletion, and waste accumulation.

LACK OF SUSTAINABLE PRACTICES

The leading causes are poor policies, weak enforcement, and short term economic focus impacts this problem and worsens conservation efforts.

ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS

BIODIVERSITY CRISIS

Species are disappearing faster than natural rates, which causes ecosystem collapse and reduced resistance to climate change.

CLIMATE CHANGE

Rising temperatures cause glaciers to melt, raise sea levels, and intensify the extreme weather conditions which threatens the communities near the shores.

HABITAT DESTRUCTION

Human activities like mining, deforestation, and urbanization destroy the natural habitats of animals which displaces them.

SOCIOECONOMIC EFFECTS

HUMAN DISPLACEMENTS

3 million people were displaced due to the effects of climate change in Lake Chad Basin

LIVELIHOODS AFFECTED

More than 1 billion farmers worldwide have their livelihoods threatened

SOLUTIONS

IN

ACTION

RENEWABLE ENERGY SOURCES

The current solutions for renewable energy sources are solar, wind, geothermal, hydroelectric, and biomass - with solar energy is currently the leading solution.

CONSERVATION EFFORTS

Current efforts include the three Rs and shifting to renewable energy. Each effort tackles a different problem like waste management and depleting energy sources.

GOVERNMENT POLICIES

Different governments are doing different polices such as giving tax holidays for renewable energy developers, fixed payments for producers, and investing in the industry.

WHAT

YOU

CAN

DO

REDUCE CONSUMPTION

We should be conscious of the amount of resources we consume not only to reduce the rate of resource depletion but also to reduce the waste produced.

SUPPORT SUSTAINABILITY

Buying green products reduce the use of non-renewable energy by using less of it. These products are energy efficient and designed to use less energy to reduce carbon footprint.

ADVOCATE FOR CHANGE

Support environmental policies, join conservation organizations, and educate others on how they can also help the cause.
